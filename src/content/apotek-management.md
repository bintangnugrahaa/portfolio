Pharmacies face unique operational challenges compared to standard retail businesses. They deal with critical inventory where expiration dates and precise stock levels are matters of health, safety, and compliance. Relying on manual bookkeeping or generic POS software often leads to overlooked expired medications, inaccurate stock counts, and slow checkout processes during peak hours. The goal was to build a specialized management system tailored to the strict daily workflows of a modern pharmacy.

To address these critical needs, I developed a comprehensive web-based Point of Sale and inventory application. The core of the system is an intuitive cashier interface designed for rapid transactions, seamlessly connected to a robust backend database. I implemented specific modules for medical inventory management, including automated alerts for soon-to-expire drugs, batch tracking, and secure role-based access for pharmacists and administrators. The architecture ensures that every time a medication is dispensed, the central inventory is updated in real-time.

The implementation of this system significantly improved both back-office management and front-end customer service. By automating expiration tracking, the pharmacy drastically reduced financial losses from unsellable stock and ensured compliance with safety standards. Furthermore, the streamlined checkout process minimized wait times for patients, while the integrated reporting dashboard provided owners with clear, actionable insights into daily sales and overall business health.
